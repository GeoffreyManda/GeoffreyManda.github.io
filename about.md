    <h2>Professional Summary</h2>
    <p>I am a PhD Research Fellow in Biostatistics and Epidemiology at Queen Mary University of London, advised by Professors Andrew Prendergast and Melanie Smuk. My PhD research focuses on establishing statistical and algorithmic foundations for discovery and decision-making in systems governed by underlying causal rules. In particular, I develop and apply tools from mathematical statistics and machine learning (ML) to understand causal relationships from data, model and extrapolate to predict the effects of interventions, and select informative interventions and/or policies for experimental or trial design.</p>

    <p>Motivated by problems in clinical care, pharmacology, and cell biology, this work aims to accelerate mechanistic discovery and translation to biomedical engineering and public health policy. My research is supported by the prestigious CREATE PhD Fellowship.</p>

    <p>I bring over eight years of experience across clinical practice and public health research, including roles with the World Health Organization (WHO) and as Research Project Manager at Kamuzu University of Health Sciences, collaborating with AMREF Health Africa, UNICEF, the World Bank Group, USAID, and the Malawi Ministry of Health to shape evidence-based policy and implement high-impact interventions in HIV, TB, Nutrition, Oncology, Maternal-Child health, and health systems.</p>
  </section>
